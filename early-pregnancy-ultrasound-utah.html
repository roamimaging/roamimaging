Early Pregnancy Ultrasound FAQ

What is the best time for an early pregnancy ultrasound?

The ideal window for early pregnancy ultrasound is 7-14 weeks gestational age. At this stage, we can detect the heartbeat, provide accurate dating, and confirm pregnancy viability while ensuring clear visualization.

Can you detect the heartbeat at 7 weeks?

Yes! The fetal heartbeat is typically detectable via ultrasound starting around 6-7 weeks gestational age. By 7-8 weeks, we can usually provide clear heartbeat detection and even audio recording for you to keep.

Is early pregnancy ultrasound safe?

Absolutely! Ultrasound technology has been used safely in pregnancy for decades. Our equipment meets all medical safety standards, and our certified sonographers follow established protocols for early pregnancy imaging.

What should I expect during my early ultrasound?

Your session will last 30-45 minutes. We'll confirm pregnancy, measure the gestational sac and embryo, detect heartbeat, provide accurate dating, and capture beautiful images for you to treasure. Everything happens in the comfort of your home.

Do you provide medical diagnosis?

Our service is for bonding and keepsake purposes. While we use medical-grade equipment and certified sonographers, we are not a substitute for prenatal medical care. We recommend continuing regular OB/GYN visits.

How quickly will I receive my images?

All ultrasound images and heartbeat recordings are sent digitally the same day as your appointment. You'll receive high-quality images via email within 2-4 hours of your session completion.
