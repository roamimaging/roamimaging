3D 4D Ultrasound FAQ

What's the best time for 3D 4D ultrasound?

The optimal window is 20-32 weeks gestational age. During this time, your baby has developed facial features but still has enough amniotic fluid around them for clear imaging. 26-30 weeks is often considered the sweet spot.

What's the difference between 3D and 4D ultrasound?

3D ultrasound creates still, three-dimensional images showing your baby's features in detail. 4D ultrasound adds real-time movement, so you can watch your baby yawn, stretch, or suck their thumb in live action video.

How long does a 3D 4D session take?

Sessions typically last 45-60 minutes. This allows time for setup, finding optimal positioning, capturing multiple angles, and ensuring we get the best possible images and videos of your baby.

What if my baby isn't cooperating for good images?

Baby positioning can affect image quality. We may suggest walking around, having a snack, or gentle belly movement to encourage baby to move into a better position. We'll work patiently to get the best possible images.

Can family members attend the session?

Absolutely! We encourage family participation. Partners, children, and grandparents can all witness this special moment. It's a wonderful bonding experience for the whole family.

How many images and videos will I receive?

You'll receive 15+ high-resolution 3D images and multiple 4D video clips captured during your session. All digital files are sent via email the same day, and you can print, share, and keep them forever.

Is 3D 4D ultrasound safe?

Yes, 3D/4D ultrasound uses the same sound waves as traditional 2D ultrasound, which has been used safely in pregnancy for decades. Our equipment meets all medical safety standards and guidelines.
